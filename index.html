Tax lien sale highlights social inequities in NYC

Myriam Vidal Valero

After the tax lien sale was cancelled in 2020 due to the pandemic, the New York City Department of Finance announced in September that this year’s sale will happen on December 17th.

NOV. 18, 2021

A building in the list sits abandoned in Sunnyside, Queens. Photo: Myriam Vidal Valero.

The sale is a mechanism in which the city sells people’s property tax debt to private trusts of investors who then collect the debt from the owners with high interests. The department of finance identifies the properties by tax lots, explained the New York City Architect Jorge Fontan. The institution then publishes a list with all the lots whose debt will be sold.

Historically, racially diverse boroughs have been the most affected, like Queens with a 75% of non-white population. Activists are working to find the property owners and help them with the paperwork to get their houses off the list. This can be difficult because, as Fontan said, “you can have one lot with many addresses, even one building can have more than one address.”
